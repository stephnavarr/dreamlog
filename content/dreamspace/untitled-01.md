---
title: "the girl"
date: 2026-07-01
excerpt: "Inspired by Jung, this poem is about the Shadow, the Self, and the Child integrating into one. I started writing this in 2023, and I do diverge from this idea as the poem goes along, shifting my focus on the inner child. My adult self dreams about wholeness, but my child self just wants someone to stay."
---

maybe we'll all dance one day
bare feet on dewy grass-
my pains
my sunshines
my strengths
my weaknesses-
maybe one day we'll creep out of the shadows
and show the world our faces.

the light 
and the dark.

we'll travel together
take inventory of new shapes,
introduce each other to the things we've lost.

there is just one room
we all still avoid.

we've talked to the woman who stayed too long
and we've talked to the one who left.
we don't know what to say
to the girl.

I crack the door open
and peer inside.

she's smaller than I remember
her long dark hair
cascading down her back,
books stacked high on her bed,
paint peeling off the walls surrounding her,
bugs crawling up and down her arms,
the ghosts of her home
circling her body.

her eyes are closed
hands clasped against her chest
whispering:

hug me,
hold me here.
look at me again
tell me that the things I’ve made are beautiful
tell me that you remember me
tell me that you see me.
tell me you won’t go away again
that you love me
that we can share the world 
like before.

maybe this time
we'll call her name
and she'll look into my eyes.
we'll take her to our center.

there'll be no lines anymore
the Earth is mine
the Earth is ours.
