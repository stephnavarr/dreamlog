September 12, 2006

the boy with the blue backpack

i memorized your schedule before i knew your name

seventh grade and i had already learned
the exact sound of your sneakers
on the linoleum

you sat two rows ahead of me
in english
and i spent all of october
studying the back of your neck
like it was something i’d be tested on

i wrote your name
in the margin of my notebook
then scribbled it out
then wrote it again smaller

this is what love was then:
proximity
and the unbearable hope
that you might turn around

you never did
but i kept the notebook

i think that says everything
about who i was at twelve
and maybe still
