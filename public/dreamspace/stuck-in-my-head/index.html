stuck in my head

I wrote this during a time when I was falling into a deep depression. I was in a relationship that skewed my sense of reality, and I was losing my grip. This is also the year I read Sylvia Plath's The Bell Jar.

there are times when my light feels dim when the words I say feel like they fall out of my mouth and thud onto the floor. there are times where i want to slip into the corners of my mind and stay there, spend my days staring at the wallks.

to know me is to know my pain- so I let those memories trickle down my throat let the pain branch through my body let that ancient storm swallow me,

stuck in my head.
